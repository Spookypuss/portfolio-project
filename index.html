Weather app

When learning to build webpages, there is little as exhilarating as hooking into your first API.

While the styling on this app wasn't a tour de force, the joy of APIs did overcome me and I was preaching the good word of APIs to everyone I know.

And as if my enthusiasm wasn't at fever-pitch already, I learned how to employ a second style sheet to give a dark theme to the app. This sent me stratispheric in a fervor of coding rapture. So it might not look like much, but it meant an awful lot to me.


World clock

There are a few touchpoints problems that virtually all students of the online arts tackle, and like the weather app, the world clock is one of those.

This project was a great experience for me, and I enjoyed trying my hand (for better or worse) at a little 90s styling.

My world clock app offers a selection of locations across the globe, as well as making an educated guess at the current time for the end user, when selected.


ABAB brewery website

As a keen amateur brewer, I have produced a number of beers in collaboration with my partner. We initially started with our own brewery names - Authoritarian Brewery and Anarchy Brewery - and abreviated these to ABAB for our collaborative persuits.

As I enjoy making beer labels almost as much as I enjoy developing recipes, and as we have been keen to share the experience with our friends and family, making a website to document our brews seemed a logical next step.

There we archive our specialty batches such as those to celebrate weddings and births (or even just the sun coming back to the garden after winter). We also have a gallery of labels to remember the greatest hits from our fermenter.
